### `scripts/prepare_solr_docs.py` — original

**Document shape (`SolrDoc`):** 10 fields — `id`, `type`, `title`, `body`, `full_text`, `subreddit`,
`score`, `created_date`, `thread_id`, `source_file`.

**Sources:** 3 topic CSVs only (`bitcoin`, `information_security`, `seo`).

**Key logic:**
- `infer_type(text)` — classified records as `post` if `len(text) > 240`, otherwise `comment`.
  Type was purely length-based with no ground-truth signal.
- `title` — first 150 characters of text for "posts", empty string for "comments".
- `full_text` — concatenation of title + body (duplicate content for posts).
- `thread_id` — 16-character SHA1 truncation of `(subreddit, posted_time, text[:50])`.
  Synthetic, not a real Reddit thread key.
- Timestamp stored as raw string from CSV — no normalisation to UTC ISO-8601.
- Deduplication key: `(full_text.lower(), created_date, subreddit)`.
- No sentiment, no model/vendor extraction, no opinion metadata.

**Output:** 2,341 docs from 3 CSVs.
